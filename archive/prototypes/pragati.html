আলো জ্বেলে যাও, ঐতিহ্যে ভাসো Where the dhaak beats,
and home comes alive.

জয় মা কালী, অভয়দায়িনী Fearless night,
sacred fire.

সরস্বতী, জ্ঞানদায়িনী The first day of spring,
the goddess of words.

Five days of light. A thousand familiar faces. Bengali Philadelphia, lit up like a village courtyard at Pujo — Pragati has been bringing this home since 2012.

The darkest night of the year, lit up by Maa's fearless presence and the bursting sky. Pragati's Kali Pujo brings the Amavasya home — fire, devotion, dhunuchi smoke, and family.

A mustard-yellow morning, white sarees and palash blooms, children placing their books at her feet. Pragati's Saraswati Pujo brings the gentlest day of the Bengali year home.

"যেখানে দশভুজার আশীর্বাদ, সেখানেই আমাদের বাড়ি।" "Where Maa Durga's blessing dwells, there we make our home."

Formed in 2012, Pragati is among the oldest Bengali organizations on the East Coast — dedicated to honouring our shared heritage while welcoming every neighbour, friend, and stranger into the warmth of a Bengali household.
